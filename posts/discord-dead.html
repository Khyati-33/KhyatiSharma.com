Is Discord Dead?

by Siddharth Duggal on December 11th, 2022

Discord is a popular chat app that is used by many people. It is used by gamers, streamers, and many other people. It is a great app, but is it dying?


COVID-19 made lots of people, including myself, switch to online chat places. I first started using Discord in 2018, however, I became a daily user when COVID struck in 2020. I used to own a Discord server with 3k members, which I deleted in 2021 as I couldn't manage it anymore due to time constraints. A lot of my online friends from Discord have gone inactive/have deleted their accounts. I still use Discord, but I definitely don't use it as much as I once did.


Well, why are less people using Discord? People are now interacting more IRL rather than online. The large majority of Discord users are under 18, and in high school. When Covid was a thing, people were stuck at home, and had nothing to do. They would spend their time on Discord. Now that people are back in school, they have less time to spend on Discord.


Tbh, the future of Discord doesn't seem too bright. They have made less revenue in 2022 than how much they made in the preceding years, 2021 and 2020. They will definitely still be profitable, but it won't be used as much as people used it in 2020. Recently, they've began adding more features to their app, and I think that it will help them. They have also been adding new "perks" for "discord nitro" users. For example, they've added a new banner feature for people to use.
